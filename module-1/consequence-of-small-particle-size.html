✨ Consequence of small particle size refers to the structural and energetic changes that arise as particle dimensions shrink to the nanoscale, where surface atoms begin to outnumber bulk atoms, fundamentally altering reactivity, stability, and thermodynamic behavior.

Quantum Confinement Effect

When the size of a material is reduced to the nanoscale (1-100 nm), its electrons become spatially confined. This quantum confinement causes the energy levels to become discrete rather than continuous, fundamentally changing the material's optical, electronic, and chemical properties.
  • Quantum confinement occurs when nanoparticle size is comparable to the de Broglie wavelength of the electron.

Band Gap & Energy Levels

💠
Conduction & Valence Band
As quantum dot size decreases, the band gap energy \(E_g\) increases. Electrons need more energy to jump from the valence band to the conduction band, causing a blue shift in the emitted wavelength.
  • \(E_g\) is band gap energy between valence and conduction band.
  • \(\lambda\) is emission wavelength.

Size-Dependent Emission Colours

The emission colour of quantum dots shifts with size. Larger dots emit at longer wavelengths (red), while smaller dots emit at shorter wavelengths (blue).
  • Large dots have a low band gap energy, corresponding to long wavelengths that produce red emission (~700 nm).
  • Medium dots have a moderate band gap energy that produces green or yellow emission (~550 nm).
  • Small dots have a high band gap energy, corresponding to short wavelengths that produce blue emission (~400 nm).

Applications, exploiting tuneable emission

  • Bioimaging
  • LEDs
  • Solar cells
  • Sensors
