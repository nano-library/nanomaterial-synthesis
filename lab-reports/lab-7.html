✨ Gold nanoparticles are synthesized by reducing chloroauric acid \((HAuCl_4)\) using sodium citrate, which also stabilizes the formed nanoparticles.

Experimental Procedure

  • Dissolve 10 mg of \(HAuCl_4\) in 100 mL distilled water.
  • Add 40 mg of sodium citrate to the 100 mL \(HAuCl_4\) solution.
  • Heat the mixture to 100 \(^{\circ}\)C with constant stirring for 15 minutes.

Lab Questions and Answers

What is the role of sodium citrate in the synthesis of gold nanoparticles?

Sodium citrate acts as both a reducing agent and a stabilizing (capping) agent.

Write the general reaction involved in the reduction of \(Au^{3+}\) to Au^{0}.

\[ Au^{3+} + 3e^{-} → Au^{0} \]

What change in color is observed during the formation of gold nanoparticles?

The solution changes from pale yellow to wine red.

Which technique is used to confirm the formation of gold nanoparticles?

UV-Visible spectroscopy is commonly used.

What is the common precursor used for the synthesis of gold nanoparticles?

Chloroauric acid (\(HAuCl_4\)) is commonly used.

What is the oxidation state of gold in \(HAuCl_4\)?

The oxidation state of gold is +3.

What is a reducing agent?

A reducing agent is a substance that donates electrons to another substance.
