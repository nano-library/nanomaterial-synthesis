✨ Synthesis of \(TiO_2\) nanoparticles is carried out using a sonochemical method, where ultrasonic waves promote hydrolysis and formation of fine, uniform particles.

Experimental Procedure

  • Take 8 mL of isopropyl alcohol in a 50 mL beaker.
  • Add 1 mL of titanium isopropoxide (TTIP) slowly under continuous stirring.
  • Add 0.2 mL of acetic acid to stabilize the solution (optional).
  • Slowly add 2 mL of distilled water dropwise to the solution while stirring.
  • A milky white suspension begins to form due to hydrolysis.
  • Place the beaker in an ultrasonic bath.
  • Sonicate the mixture at 40 kHz for 30 minutes.
  • Maintain room temperature throughout the process.
  • After sonication, allow the solution to stand for 30 minutes.
  • A white precipitate of TiO₂ nanoparticles is formed.
  • Centrifuge the solution at 4000 rpm for 10 minutes.
  • Wash the precipitate with distilled water and IPA to remove impurities.
  • Dry the obtained powder in a hot air oven at 80 \(^{\circ}\)C for 2 hours.
  • Calcine the dried powder at 400 \(^{\circ}\)C for 2 hours to improve crystallinity.

Lab Questions and Answers

What is sonochemical synthesis? Explain the principle behind it.

Sonochemical synthesis uses ultrasonic waves to drive chemical reactions. It works on acoustic cavitation, where bubbles form and collapse, creating high temperature and pressure that promote reactions.

What is the role of ultrasonic waves in nanoparticle synthesis?

Ultrasonic waves help in mixing, nucleation, and controlling particle size by generating cavitation and localized energy.

Why is titanium dioxide (TiO₂) considered an important functional material? Mention any two applications.

\(TiO_2\) is stable, non-toxic, and has good photocatalytic properties. It is used in solar cells and environmental purification.

Why does the solution turn milky white during the experiment?

The solution turns milky due to the formation of TiO₂ nanoparticles through hydrolysis, which scatter light.

What is the role of ultrasonic irradiation in controlling nanoparticle formation?

It controls particle size and prevents aggregation by providing uniform energy and mixing.

What would happen if the experiment were carried out without sonication?

Larger and uneven particles may form due to poor mixing and lack of controlled nucleation.

How does the sonochemical method influence the size of nanoparticles compared to conventional methods?

It produces smaller and more uniform nanoparticles due to rapid nucleation and controlled growth.
