✨ \(FeS_2\) thin films are grown on a glass/FTO substrate via a hydrothermal route.
Pyrite \((FeS_2)\) is an important iron sulfide semiconductor with applications in photovoltaics, sensors, catalysis, and energy storage due to its suitable band gap, earth abundance, and non-toxicity.

Experimental Procedure

  • Preparation of Seed Solution

    • Weigh 0.668 g of Sodium Thiosulfate Pentahydrate \((Na_2S_2O_3\cdot 5H_2O)\) and 0.167 g of Ferrous Chloride Tetrahydrate \((FeCl_2 \cdot 4H_2O)\).
    • Then, dropwise add 14ml of ethanol to the above solution, the temperature of the solution gradually increased up to 80 \(^\circ\)C.
    • The pH of the solution should be within the range 2 to 3.
    • Stir the solution for 40-45 min until gradual evaporation and sol-gel solution is obtained.
    • Remove the substrate and allow it to dry naturally.
    • Oven-dry the product at 80 \(^\circ\)C for 3 h and allow it to cool to room temperature.
    • A dry white powder is obtained.
    • Finally, grind the powder using mortar-pestle.

  • Preparation of Nutrient Solution

    • Dissolve 2 g of Sodium Thiosulfate Pentahydrate \((Na_2S_2O_3\cdot 5H_2O)\) and 0.5 g of Ferrous Chloride Tetrahydrate \((FeCl_2 \cdot 4H_2O)\) in 30 mL of DI water.
    • Stir the solution for 1 hour until the solution turns black, indicating the formation of iron sulfide complexes.

  • Hydrothermal Growth

    • Transfer the nutrient solution into a 50 mL Teflon-lined autoclave.
    • Place the seed-coated substrate vertically inside the autoclave.
    • Seal the autoclave properly and heat it at 200 \(^\circ\)C for 20 hours.
    • Allow the autoclave to cool naturally to room temperature.
    • Remove the substrate and rinse gently with DI water.

  • Annealing

    • Anneal the as-grown FeS₂ film at 80 \(^\circ\)C for 1 hour to improve film adhesion and crystallinity.

Lab Questions and Answers

💠
Why are sodium thiosulfate pentahydrate \((Na_2S_2O_3 \cdot 5H_2O)\) and ferrous chloride tetrahydrate \((FeCl_2 \cdot 4H_2O)\) used as precursors for pyrite \((FeS_2)\)?

Sodium thiosulfate pentahydrate \((Na_2S_2O_3 \cdot 5H_2O)\) serves as the source of sulfur, while ferrous chloride tetrahydrate \((FeCl_2 \cdot 4H_2O)\) provides iron(II) ions \((Fe^{2+})\). Under hydrothermal conditions (elevated temperature and pressure), thiosulfate decomposes to release sulfur species, which react with \(Fe^{2+}\) to form pyrite \((FeS_2)\). The choice of these precursors is based on their high solubility in water, stability at room temperature, and ability to undergo controlled decomposition to yield phase-pure pyrite without unwanted byproducts like hematite \((Fe_2O_3)\) or magnetite \((Fe_3O_4)\).

💠
Why is ethanol added when increasing the temperature?

Ethanol is added to prevent agglomeration of pyrite nanoparticles. It also helps in removing water molecules from the precursor mixture, promoting the formation of pure pyrite phase by avoiding oxidation to iron oxides like hematite \((Fe_2O_3)\) or goethite \((FeOOH)\). Additionally, ethanol acts as a mild reducing agent, maintaining the sulfur-rich environment necessary for pyrite formation at elevated temperatures.

💠
Why does the nutrient solution turn black during stirring?

The nutrient solution turns black due to the formation of amorphous iron sulfide (FeS) or fine pyrite \((FeS_2)\) nanoparticles that nucleate rapidly during stirring before hydrothermal treatment.

What role does the seed layer play in thin film growth?

The seed layer provides nucleation sites that promote uniform, adherent, and oriented thin film growth by reducing the activation energy for deposition.

💠
How does temperature affect hydrothermal crystal growth?

Temperature affects hydrothermal crystal growth by controlling solubility, reaction rate, nucleation density, and crystal size. Higher temperature increases solubility and reaction rate, leading to faster growth but may also promote larger or more defective crystals. Lower temperature favors slower, more controlled nucleation, often yielding smaller, more uniform crystals.

💠
Why is annealing performed after hydrothermal synthesis?

Annealing is performed to remove defects, improve crystallinity, and convert metastable phases (e.g., hydroxide to oxide) by providing thermal energy for atomic rearrangement.
