✨ The polycrystalline \(Cu(OH)_2\) nanowires were synthesized by a simple two-step process in aqueous Ammonia \((NH_3)\) and Sodium hydroxide \((NaOH)\) solutions. 1D \(Cu(OH)_2\) nanostructures have been employed as the precursors for the fabrication of 1D \(CuO\) nanostructures.
\(Cu(OH)_2\) and basic \(Cu(II)\) salts with an orthorhombic structure have received much attention in recent years. 1D \(Cu(OH)_2\) nanomaterials have been fabricated through solution-phase chemical approaches involving coordination self-assembly.
The transformation from 1D \(Cu(OH)_2\) nanostructures to a variety of novel CuO nanostructures (wires, platelets, leaflets, and flowers) has been achieved by thermal dehydration of the as-prepared \(Cu(OH)_2\) solution or in the solid state.

Experimental Procedure

  • Preparation of \(Cu(OH)_2\) nanostructures

    • 0.8ml of 1M \(KOH\) solution was first dropped into 2ml of 0.1M \(CuSO_4\) solution under vigorous stirring at room temperature.
    • The above process is followed by dropping 0.3ml of ammonia solution.
    • After 3min of further stirring, the mixed solution was kept static in a sealed vessel for 12h.
    • The resultant precipitate was filtered off, washed thoroughly with deionised water, and dried in a vacuum at 25 \(^\circ\)C for 12h.

  • Preparation of \(CuO\) Nanowires

    • For the dehydration of \(Cu(OH)_2\) nanostructures in solution, the \(Cu(OH)_2\) nanostructures formed immediately after adding both alkaline solutions were heated at a given temperature (e.g., 30 and 50 \(^\circ\)C) for 12 h.
    • The as-prepared \(Cu(OH)_2\) nanostructures were separated and redispersed in deionised water, followed by heat treatment at 70 \(^\circ\)C for 12h.
    • For the dehydration of \(Cu(OH)_2\) nanostructures in the solid state, the as-prepared \(Cu(OH)_2\) nanostructures were heated at 120 \(^\circ\)C for 2h to dehydrate and subsequently at 180 \(^\circ\)C for another 3 h to promote crystallization.

Lab Questions and Answers

💠
Why is Copper(II) sulfate used as a precursor for \(Cu(OH)_2\) nanostructures?

\(CuSO_4\) is used because it is a readily soluble, stable, and cost-effective source of \(Cu^{2+}\) ions, which easily react with OH⁻ (from \(NaOH\) or \(KOH\)) to precipitate \(Cu(OH)_2\) nanostructures.

💠
What is the role of ammonia \((NH_3)\) solution in nanostructure synthesis?

Ammonia solution acts as a complexing agent and shape-directing agent. It forms a stable copper-ammine complex \([Cu(NH_3)_4]^{2+}\) , which slowly releases \(Cu^{2+}\) ions to control nucleation and growth, promoting anisotropic, one-dimensional, nanowire formation.

💠
Why is \(NaOH\) (or \(KOH\)) used in the synthesis of \(Cu(OH)_2\) nanowires?

\(NaOH\) (or \(KOH\)) provides \(OH^-\) ions that react with \(Cu^{2+}\) to precipitate \(Cu(OH)_2\). The high pH also promotes anisotropic growth along the [010] direction, favoring nanowire formation.
