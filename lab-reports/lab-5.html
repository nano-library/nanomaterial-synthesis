✨ There are several ways to prepare aluminium nanoparticles, one is the Sol-Gel method.

Experimental Procedure

  • Prepare 0.18M of Aluminum Nitrate Nonahydrate \(Al(NO_3)_3 \cdot 9H_2O\) by completely dissolving in 150ml of Deionized water using the magnetic stirrer at room temperature.
  • Then, dropwise add 14ml of ethanol to the above solution, the temperature of the solution gradually increased up to 80 \(^\circ\)C.
  • The pH of the solution should be within the range 2 to 3.
  • Stir the solution for 40-45 min until gradual evaporation and sol-gel solution is obtained.
  • Oven-dry the product at 80 \(^\circ\)C for 3 h and allow it to cool to room temperature.
  • A dry white powder is obtained.
  • Finally, grind the powder using mortar-pestle

Characterization of the Obtained Powder

  • Obtain the XRD spectra of the synthesized \(Al_2O_3\) nanoparticles. Analyse the synthesized \(Al_2O_3\) nanoparticles using BET and FESEM technique.
  • Calculate the crystallite size of the synthesized nanoparticles from the XRD spectra using Debye Scherrer equation:
    \[ D = \frac{k\lambda}{\beta \cos\theta} \]
    Where, D - Crystallite size, k - shape factor (0.9), \(\lambda\) - X-ray wavelength, \(\theta\) - peak position, \(\beta\) - peak width at FWHM (in radians).
  • Measure the surface area and porosity of the \(Al_2O_3\) nanoparticles.
  • Analyse the morphology of the synthesized nanoparticles using SEM images and calculate the elemental composition.

Lab Questions and Answers

Name the common precursors used for the synthesis of \(Al_2O_3\) nanoparticles.

The most commonly used precursors are aluminium nitrate \((Al(NO_3)_3 \cdot 9H_2O)\), aluminium isopropoxide \((Al(OC_3H_7)_3)\), and aluminium chloride \((AlCl_3)\). These are preferred because they dissolve easily in solvents and undergo controlled hydrolysis, which is essential for the sol-gel process.

Chemical reaction for the conversion of aluminium hydroxide to aluminium oxide

When aluminium hydroxide is heated (a process called calcination), it loses water molecules and converts to aluminium oxide. The reaction is: \[ 2 Al(OH)_3 \rightarrow Al_2O_3 + 3H_2O \]

Which techniques are commonly used to characterize \(Al_2O_3\) nanoparticles?

XRD (X-ray diffraction) reveals the crystal structure and crystallite size.
BET (Brunauer-Emmett-Teller) analysis measures the surface area and porosity, critical properties since nanoparticles derive much of their usefulness from their high surface area.
FESEM (Field Emission Scanning Electron Microscopy) lets you directly visualize the morphology and particle shape.
EDAX/EDS (Energy Dispersive X-ray Spectroscopy), often coupled with SEM, confirms the elemental composition of the sample.

What information does XRD provide about \(Al_2O_3\) nanoparticles?

XRD works by shining X-rays onto the sample and measuring how they diffract (scatter) off the crystal planes. It tells you the crystal phase (for example, whether you have alpha-alumina or gamma-alumina, which have very different properties), the crystallinity of the sample, and crucially, the crystallite size, calculated using the Debye-Scherrer equation: \[ D = \frac{k \lambda}{\beta \cos{\theta}} \]

What are the applications of \(Al_2O_3\)?

Aluminium oxide is remarkably versatile. In ceramics and refractories, its high melting point \((\sim 2050^{\circ} C)\) and hardness make it ideal for furnace linings and cutting tools. In optics, it is used to make scratch-resistant coatings and synthetic sapphires (which are simply single-crystal \(Al_2O_3\)). In catalysis, it acts as a support material for catalysts in petroleum refining. At the nanoscale specifically, \(Al_2O_3\) nanoparticles are used in drug delivery, water purification (adsorbing heavy metals and fluoride), and as abrasives in polishing semiconductors and optical surfaces.
